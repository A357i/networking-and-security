Room: Passive Reconnaissance
Platform: TryHackMe — Network Security Module

What did I learn?
- Passive recon = publicly available sources only, no packets to target
- Active recon = direct target interaction, detectable, requires explicit authorisation
- whois/RDAP reveals registrar, dates, nameservers (personal data now usually redacted)
- WHOIS is being replaced by RDAP — use curl + jq for modern structured output
- dig is the preferred DNS tool; nslookup is legacy but still encountered
- DNS record types: A, AAAA, MX, CNAME, TXT, SOA — each reveals different infrastructure
- TXT records are high-value: SPF gaps = email spoofing risk; CTF flags sometimes hidden here
- DNSDumpster finds subdomains and produces a visual relationship map
- crt.sh (CT logs) is the most effective passive subdomain discovery method — 10-100x more than DNSDumpster
- Shodan indexes internet-connected device banners — reveals open ports, service versions, and org info without touching the target
- Passive recon triggers no alerts and carries minimal legal risk but still requires engagement authorisation
