- When you see a scope involving web applications, your first thought should be OWASP WSTG
- When a client is in payments/finance, compliance requirements override framework preference
- MITRE ATT&CK technique IDs in a report give the blue team actionable detection guidance — always consider annotating findings
- ISSAF's nine steps (recon → network map → vuln ID → penetration → privesc → enumerate → lateral move → persist → cover tracks) mirror exactly how real attacks progress
- "Checklist mentality" is a real failure mode in WSTG-style testing — always assess overall risk posture, not just tick boxes
- PTES pre-engagement phase detail is the part most junior testers skip and get wrong — scope creep and legal disputes start here
