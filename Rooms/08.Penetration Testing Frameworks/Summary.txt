What did I learn?
- Penetration testing frameworks solve the problem of disorganized, non-repeatable testing
- Five major frameworks covered: OSSTMM, OWASP WSTG, NIST SP 800-115, PTES, ISSAF
- MITRE ATT&CK is a complementary knowledge base, not a framework
- Framework selection is always context-driven — no single framework fits all engagements
- Real engagements use a primary framework + supplementary frameworks
- ISSAF is outdated but its nine-step adversarial progression model remains valuable for understanding attacker logic
- PTES is the most practical framework for standard corporate pentests
- OSSTMM is uniquely quantitative but requires specialist knowledge
- Next rooms in module: Threat Modeling for Pentesters, Planning and Scoping
- These will formalize adversarial mindset alongside the methodological discipline built here
