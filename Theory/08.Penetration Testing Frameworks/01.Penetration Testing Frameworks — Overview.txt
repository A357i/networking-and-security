A penetration testing framework is a structured methodology that guides a tester through every stage of an engagement — from scoping to reporting. Without one, testing becomes disorganized, coverage is incomplete, and results are not repeatable or defensible.
Four benefits of using a framework:

Thoroughness — critical areas are not overlooked
Consistency — different testers produce comparable results
Compliance — aligns with regulatory requirements
Communication — clients and auditors trust a recognized standard

Framework selection is always context-driven. The right choice depends on: engagement scope and target type, regulatory obligations, need for quantifiable results, and team expertise.
