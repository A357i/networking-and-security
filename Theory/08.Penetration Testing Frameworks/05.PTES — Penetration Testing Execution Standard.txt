Available at pentest-standard.org
Developed by experienced practitioners to define what a real pentest looks like end-to-end
Focuses on how the engagement flows, not what to test or how to measure
Seven phases:

Pre-Engagement Interactions — scope, rules of engagement, authorization letter, emergency contacts, testing windows
Intelligence Gathering — passive recon (LinkedIn, certificate transparency, job postings) and active recon (DNS enum, network scanning)
Threat Modeling — identify highest-value assets, define primary attack paths using adversarial logic
Vulnerability Analysis — automated scanning + manual verification to eliminate false positives
Exploitation — purposeful exploitation to demonstrate business impact, not just "pop shells"
Post-Exploitation — pivot, extract credentials, demonstrate lateral movement; translate technical access into business risk
Reporting — executive summary (business risk in plain language) + technical report (exact steps, affected hosts, evidence, remediation)


Best for: standard corporate network pentests, junior testers building workflow instincts
Weakness: not formally updated in several years; tool-specific guidance is outdated. Supplement with current documentation.
