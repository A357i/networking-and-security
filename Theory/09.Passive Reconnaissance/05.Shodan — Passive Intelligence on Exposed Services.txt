Shodan is a search engine for internet-connected devices. It continuously scans the public internet, collects banners and responses from open ports, and indexes them. Unlike Google (which indexes web page content), Shodan indexes devices: servers, IoT equipment, cameras, routers, industrial control systems.
What a Shodan host entry shows:

IP address and ASN (Autonomous System Number — identifies the network block)
Hosting provider/organisation (e.g., Cloudflare, AWS)
Geographic location (country, city)
Open ports and services with version strings and banners
Tags: cdn, vuln (if a known vulnerability matches the detected service version)

Shodan search filters:
hostname:tryhackme.com        → match specific hostname
org:"TryHackMe"               → filter by organisation name
port:443 country:US           → filter by port and country
http.component:"wordpress"    → identify technology stack

Defensive value: organisations use Shodan to identify their own unintended exposures — rogue servers, forgotten test machines, vulnerable services indexed before anyone noticed.
Key passive recon insight: Shodan reveals what your target looks like from the internet before you send a single packet. Feed IPs from dig/nslookup into Shodan to see what services are running and what version banners are exposed.
